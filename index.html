Frequently Asked Questions

How do currency exchange rates work?

Exchange rates are determined by supply and demand in the foreign exchange market. They fluctuate based on economic factors like inflation, interest rates, political stability, and trade balances between countries.

What is the best way to convert currency with low fees?

Use online currency converters for rate comparisons, avoid airport exchanges (they charge 10-15% markup), consider multi-currency cards like Wise or Revolut, and convert larger amounts at once to minimize per-transaction fees.

How often do exchange rates change?

Exchange rates change constantly during forex market hours (24 hours a day, 5 days a week). Our converter updates every 60 seconds to provide near real-time rates from the European Central Bank.

What is cost-of-living adjustment for salaries?

Cost-of-living adjustment compares the purchasing power between cities. A $100,000 salary in New York might equal $60,000 in Bangkok in terms of what you can afford, due to differences in housing, food, and services costs.

How does inflation affect currency value?

Inflation reduces purchasing power over time. $100 in 2000 would need to be about $180 today to buy the same goods, due to cumulative inflation of roughly 80%. Our inflation calculator helps you understand this impact.
